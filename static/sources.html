Sources

Last reviewed: June 2026

SleepWise draws on public, government-maintained medical databases. Evidence shown on each result links back to one of these.

Primary sources

  • NIH Office of Dietary Supplements (ODS) - fact sheets on supplements such as melatonin, magnesium, and valerian. ods.od.nih.gov
  • MedlinePlus (U.S. National Library of Medicine) - consumer information on herbs and supplements. medlineplus.gov
  • openFDA - drug label and adverse-event data from the FDA. open.fda.gov
  • RxNorm (U.S. National Library of Medicine) - standard drug naming, used to normalize medication names. nlm.nih.gov

How sources are used

Dose ranges and evidence summaries are drawn from the fact sheets above. Each supplement's evidence is tagged with whether it has been verified against its cited source; unverified entries are flagged in the data and are being reviewed. Interaction rules are hand-built from these sources and labeled with their basis.

What sources do not replace

These are reference materials, not personal medical advice. Your clinician or pharmacist has context that no public database can. See the Medical disclaimer.
